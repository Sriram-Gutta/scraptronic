About Scraptronic

I'm Sriram, a computer engineering student at Purdue from San Diego. I built Scraptronic because I had a drawer of dead phones and old laptop batteries and no idea what to do with them. The official lists are scattered across half a dozen government sites, the scrap prices live on industry pages, and nobody connects "you have an old laptop" to "this aluminum and copper is actually worth a few dollars."

This project pulls those pieces together for the San Diego area: a single map of recyclers, honest estimates of what materials are worth, and a way to track what you've kept out of a landfill. Everything's open source.

The frontend is plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with Leaflet for the map. The backend is a small Python Flask API. Recycler data comes from public CalRecycle, San Diego ESD, and EPA listings — every entry cites its source.
