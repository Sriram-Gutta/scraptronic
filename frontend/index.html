How It Actually Works

Three steps. No accounts, no payments, no friction.

1

Find a place

Open the recycler map. Pick a drop-off near you, see what they accept and when they're open. Most San Diego recyclers take e-waste for free because California's SB 20 program subsidizes them.

2

See what it's worth

Punch your materials into the calculator — say, 5 lbs of copper or 10 lbs of mixed boards. You get a dollar estimate based on current regional scrap prices, with the source cited.

3

Track your impact

Log each drop-off in the tracker. Watch your pounds, dollars, points, and CO₂ saved add up — all using EPA WARM model factors. Everything stays in your browser.

Why I Built This

San Diego, a drawer of dead phones, and an unanswered question.

I'm a computer engineering student from San Diego with a drawer of dead phones and old laptop chargers that I'd been telling myself I'd "figure out" for two years. When I finally tried, the recycling itself wasn't the problem — the information was. It was scattered across CalRecycle, the City's hazardous-waste page (which doesn't even take e-waste), Earth911, and a dozen recycler websites. Scraptronic is my attempt to bundle all of that into one place.
